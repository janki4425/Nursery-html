Characteristics of Climbers

  • Climbers are weak-stemmed plants that cannot stand upright on their own.
  • They need support to grow upward — such as sticks, walls, fences, or other plants.
  • Have special structures like tendrils, hooks, or twining stems to hold onto support
  • They "climb" to get more sunlight for better growth.
  • Many climbers produce beautiful flowers and are used in decorative gardens.
  • Some climbers are twining plants — they wrap around support with their stem (e.g., money plant).

Fun Facts About Climbers

  • Climbers can "climb" walls, trees, and fences using special parts like tendrils, hooks, or twining stems.
  • They don’t stand upright on their own — they rely on support just like someone climbing a ladder!
  • Some climbers like the grapevine have been grown for thousands of years for fruit and wine.
  • The money plant is a popular indoor climber believed to bring good luck and wealth.
